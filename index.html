The problem

Every day starts with a hundred tiny decisions.

What to eat, what to wear, when to buy cat litter, whether today is too full. None are hard — but together they drain your attention before the real day even begins. That's decision fatigue.

"What should I eat today?"
"When do I need to reorder that?"
"Today's packed — what can wait?"
The idea

Not a smarter calendar — a lighter head.

DecideLess splits your life into two things: Tasks you need to do, and Decisions made ahead of time so you don't have to think about them. You talk to it in plain language; it does the scheduling, the reasoning, and the remembering.

Talk, don't fill forms

"Water the plants every 3 days," "remind me to call the dentist Tuesday 3pm." It parses time, type, and reminders for you.

Scheduling with reasoning

Reorders supplies with lead time, builds sustainable plans, looks things up online, and rebalances an overloaded day.

Tasks vs Decisions

Tasks get a checkbox and reminders. Decisions (meals, outfits) are just shown — pre-made, so you stop deciding.

Voice, transcribed

Speak instead of type — with a live "listening" waveform, transcribed in the browser.

Plans that run themselves

"Keep my place tidy without much effort" becomes a weekly plan you can tweak, then add in one tap.

Reminders that reach you

Web-push notifications and an installable app, so the right nudge finds you at the right time.

How it feels

You suggest. It drafts. You confirm.

Every AI change is a proposal you approve — never a surprise. And anything it does, you can undo.

Under the hood

Designed and built end to end.

A deliberately minimal, low-dependency stack — a custom "quiet" design system, a real AI tool-calling loop with a draft→confirm→undo safety pattern, and hand-built calendar, auth, and reminders.

Design principles

A tool that reduces load can't create load.

Never make the user maintain data. Observe, don't interrogate. Reduce thinking, not add steps. Every screen is calm by default — one accent color, generous space, and only the next decision in view.

DecideLess — a concept project by Amy. Interactive demo; the assistant here is a lightweight on-device parser (the live app uses Claude). Nothing leaves your browser.
